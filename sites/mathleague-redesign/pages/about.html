About The Math League

Nearly five decades of inspiring young minds through the joy and challenge of mathematical problem-solving.

Our Mission

Encouraging student interest and confidence in mathematics

The Math League provides math contests for students in grades 4 through 12, as well as Algebra 1 students. Over one million students from the United States and Canada participate each year.

Every contest includes questions from different areas of mathematics. The goal is to encourage student interest and confidence through solving worthwhile problems. Many students first develop their love for mathematics through problem-solving activities like these contests.

Schools compete in statewide or multistate league competitions, fostering both individual excellence and team collaboration.

Our Founders

Presidential Award recipients for Excellence in Mathematics Teaching

In October 1985, both founders were honored by President Ronald Reagan — Mr. Conrad as the winner from New York, and Mr. Flegler as the winner from New Jersey.

SC

Steven R. Conrad

Co-Founder

Mr. Conrad taught at Roslyn High School, Roslyn, New York, from 1980 to 1998, and previously at Benjamin Cardozo High School and Francis Lewis High School in New York.

He received his B.S. from Queens College and M.S. from Yeshiva University, with additional graduate study at the University of San Francisco, Fordham University, and St. John's University.

More than 60 of his students have been named to the honors group of the Intel National Science Talent Search for mathematics papers. Six of them finished in the top 10 nationwide. His sons are both math professors.

He served as Problem Editor for The Mathematics Student Journal (NCTM) and contributed to Mathematics Magazine, The Mathematics Teacher, American Mathematical Monthly, and Crux Mathematicorum. He also served six years on the committee developing the SAT II for the College Board.

DF

Daniel Flegler

Co-Founder

Mr. Flegler taught at Waldwick High School, Waldwick, New Jersey, from 1965 to 1991, serving as department chairman for 11 years.

He received his B.A. from Brown University and M.A. from Columbia University, with additional graduate work at Fairleigh Dickinson University, the University of Iowa, and Rutgers University.

In 1977, Mr. Flegler was awarded Princeton University's prestigious award for "Distinguished Secondary School Teaching."

More than 15 of his students were named to the honors group of the Intel National Science Talent Search, with three finishing in the top 40 nationwide. He served on the Executive Committee of the Association of Mathematics Teachers of New Jersey from 1972–78.

Together, Mr. Conrad and Mr. Flegler have co-authored 18 books and have been preparing contests for math students across North America since 1977. Their work continues to impact generations of students who discover their passion for mathematics through Math League competitions.
