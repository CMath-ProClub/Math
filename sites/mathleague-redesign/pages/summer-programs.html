Summer Programs

World-class mathematics experiences — from our residential tournament at The College of New Jersey to our global online challenge.

Flagship Program

International Summer Tournament & Math Camp

Since 2012, Math League has hosted an annual summer program for students from China, Canada, the United States, and other countries. Held at The College of New Jersey in Ewing Township, NJ, this intensive program combines competitive mathematics with academic enrichment.

The program features six days of contests — individual questions, team questions, relay questions, and speed questions — alongside a series of lectures on diverse mathematical topics by speakers from Princeton, Columbia, Rutgers, Swarthmore, and Williams College.

Last summer, approximately 250 students from China, Japan, South Korea, Australia, Canada, and the United States attended.

Session 1: Grades 4 & 5

Dates: July 12–17, 2025
Location: The College of New Jersey, Ewing, NJ
Eligibility: Score of 25+ on grade-level contests

Session 2: Grades 6, 7, 8 & 9

Dates: July 21–26, 2025
Location: The College of New Jersey, Ewing, NJ
Eligibility: Score of 28+ on grade-level or 25+ on Algebra 1

Eligibility Requirements

Grades 4 & 5

A score of 25 or higher on our contests for those grade levels.

Grades 6, 7 & 8

A score of 28 or higher on grade-level contests, or 25 or higher on the Algebra 1 contest.

Grade 9

A cumulative score of 28 or higher on our six high school contests, or 25 or higher on the Algebra 1 contest.

Exceptional Students

We also consider applications from students who have not taken our contests but have demonstrated mathematical talent through other activities.

Students may use scores from previous school years to qualify. Only students currently in grades 4–9 are eligible.

Open to All Students

International Summer Challenge (Online)

In addition to our residential tournament, we host an annual online challenge open to all interested students worldwide. Seven different levels ensure students compete at an appropriate difficulty.

Format: Short Answer Contest (60 min) + Speed Round Contest (45 min)

Duration: 12:00 PM – 2:00 PM Eastern Time

Fee: $66 (USD) per grade level

Eligibility: Open to all students — no qualifying score needed
